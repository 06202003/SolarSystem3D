Mars Research Mission

The Mars Exploration Program studies Mars as a planetary system in order to understand the formation and early evolution of Mars as a planet, the history of geological processes that have shaped Mars through time, the potential for Mars to have hosted life, and the future exploration of Mars by humans. The strategy has evolved as we have learned more about Mars and as more questions have arisen. We have gone from “Follow the Water” to “Explore Habitability” to “Seek Signs of Life.”

Mars Rover 2

"Mars Rover 2, with ID 'mars_rover_2', is currently in active status and located at geographic coordinates -4.5895 latitude and 137.4417 longitude. The rover's battery currently has 78% remaining."

"The rover's sensors recorded a temperature of around -23.5 degrees Celsius, humidity of 14%, and atmospheric pressure of around 743.2 Pa. The rover's camera is currently directional with a viewing angle of 30 degrees horizontally, -15 degrees vertically, and a zoom level of 5."

"During previous missions, Mars Rover 2 has successfully collected data including 124 images, 18 soil samples and 37 atmospheric readings. Currently, the rover is carrying out a mission with the ID 'msn_001', starting on May 15, 2023, with the aim of exploring the Gale Crater area, collecting soil samples, and monitoring atmospheric conditions."

"The next mission, with the ID 'msn_002', will begin on January 10, 2024, the focus will be to conduct surveys in Mariner Valley, taking high-resolution images of geological formations, and conduct experiments on the soil of Mars."

Mars Perseverance Rover

"The Mars Perseverance Rover, identified by the ID 'perseverance_rover', is currently operating successfully on the surface of Mars. The rover has a current location at approximately -18.4444 latitude and 77.5555 longitude on the Red planet."

"The rover's battery is currently at 85% charge level, ensuring smooth operations during ongoing exploration missions."

"The Mars Perseverance sensor system recorded environmental conditions with a temperature of around -20.8 degrees Celsius, humidity of 12%, and atmospheric pressure of around 721.3 Pa."

"The final rover camera was set to a horizontal viewing angle of 25 degrees, -10 degree vertical angle, and 4 zoom levels, allowing for immersive and detailed shots."

"During its missions so far, the Mars Perseverance Rover has successfully collected valuable data, including 150 high-quality images, 20 soil samples, and 45 atmospheric readings. The most recent mission, with the ID 'msn_003', begins on December 5, 2022, and aims to explore the Jezero Crater region, collecting rock samples and looking for signs of primitive life."

Mars 3 Spacecraft

"Mars 3 Spacecraft, sebuah misi luar angkasa yang bersejarah, dikenal sebagai pionir dalam eksplorasi Mars. Diluncurkan pada 28 Mei 1971, ini adalah bagian dari program luar angkasa Soviet dan menjadi salah satu pesawat ruang angkasa pertama yang berhasil mencapai permukaan Mars."

"Pesawat ruang angkasa ini dilengkapi dengan instrumen canggih untuk mempelajari kondisi atmosfer dan geologi planet Merah. Pada tanggal 2 Desember 1971, Mars 3 berhasil mendarat di Mars, menjadikannya wahana antariksa pertama yang mencapai keberhasilan tersebut."

"Baterai Mars 3 Spacecraft menyediakan daya untuk melakukan serangkaian eksperimen dan pengukuran. Sayangnya, komunikasi dengan pesawat ruang angkasa ini terputus setelah hanya beberapa detik dari waktu pendaratan, dan pesawat ruang angkasa tersebut tidak dapat terhubung lagi."

"Meskipun misi Mars 3 berakhir secara mendadak, pencapaiannya tetap menjadi tonggak sejarah dalam eksplorasi antariksa. Data yang berhasil dikumpulkan memberikan wawasan awal yang berharga tentang planet Mars dan membantu membentuk pemahaman kita tentang planet tersebut."

"Mars 3 Spacecraft adalah saksi sejarah dari upaya manusia untuk menyelidiki rahasia dan misteri yang tersembunyi di luar angkasa, dan warisan pengetahuannya masih berkontribusi pada penelitian dan pemahaman kita tentang Tata Surya."
